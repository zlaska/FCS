# Contributing to FCS

FCS aims to be widely adopted and collaboratively improved. Anyone can propose improvements—whether you're a technologist, freelancer, HR professional, lawyer, or just someone who's used FCS and wants to share your experience.

## Ways to Propose Improvements

### Issues (for discussion)
Open an Issue to:
- Share specific scenarios from your use (the standard didn't cover something, caused ambiguity, or led to a dispute)
- Propose ideas for new sections
- Ask about the original intent of a clause
- Share a real-world case study

### Pull Requests (for concrete changes)
Open a PR to:
- Fix typos or ambiguous phrasing
- Add clauses (please discuss in an Issue first to avoid duplicate work)
- Submit translations

## Principles for Improvements

When proposing improvements, please try to follow these principles:

1. **Simplicity first**: If one sentence works, don't write two
2. **Methods over micro-details**: Specify direction; avoid locking down specific formats
3. **Inclusive**: Consider applicability across industries and team sizes
4. **Keep the human touch**: FCS is not a cold contract; phrasing should be warm and readable
5. **Stability**: Adjustments to core structure (contribution measurement, exit/entry, dispute handling) need to be cautious, as they affect all teams that have already adopted FCS

## Versioning

Semantic versioning is used:

| Change Type | Version Change | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Major structural change | Major +1 | v1.0 → v2.0 |
| Add/remove clauses, no breaking changes | Minor +1 | v1.0 → v1.1 |
| Typo fixes, phrasing tweaks | Patch +1 | v1.0 → v1.0.1 |

## Translations

Contributions of other-language versions are welcome. Translation file naming convention:
- English: `protocol.en.md`
- Japanese: `protocol.ja.md`
- Other languages: `protocol.{ISO 639 code}.md`

Place them in the repo root and add links in the README.

## Code of Conduct

- Be friendly, professional, focus on issues not individuals
- Assume good faith: a disagreement may come from a different practical experience, not personal hostility
- Data and case studies are more persuasive than abstract argument
- Even if your proposal isn't accepted, you're welcome to fork your own variant—FCS encourages many variations

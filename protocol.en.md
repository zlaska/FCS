# Flexible Cooperation Standard FCS v1.0
**灵活就业共创规范**

---

## Purpose

This standard aims to bring together people with free time and let them put their talents to use—offering a simple method of cooperation for the unemployed, freelancers, those with spare time, and those looking to change careers or contribute to public good.

Don't let idle time go to waste. Don't let people with ideas have to go it alone. Success would be great—but even if a project doesn't ultimately turn a profit, participants will have truly invested in something meaningful.

It is not a corporate charter. It is not a legal contract. It is a **default rule set for collaboration**: when a group temporarily comes together to do something, they can simply declare "we adopt FCS" and skip having to renegotiate, every time, how contributions are counted, how rewards are split, and how members come and go.

FCS applies to **the cooperation phase before a project becomes incorporated**. Once a team decides to form a formal company or organization, this standard naturally hands off to a corporate governance framework.

---

## I. Cooperation Vision

When a project starts, the initiator writes a brief **Cooperation Vision**: what to do, and why.

The vision anchors all contributions, preventing the team from drifting away from its original intent.

---

## II. Team Formation and Flow

1. **Initial Team**: At project start, the initiator sets the team's initial size and member structure.
2. **Early Joining**: Before the team reaches the agreed size, joining barriers are low to facilitate gathering talent (the specific method is decided by the initiator, e.g., direct invitation, open application).
3. **Joining After Full Roster**: After the team reaches the agreed size, new members require simple majority approval (>50%) from current members, one person one vote.
4. **Exiting**: Members may leave at any time.
5. **Permanent Ownership of Contribution Points**: Once earned, contribution points belong to the individual permanently. They are not cleared, expired, or invalidated upon exit.

---

## III. Measuring Contribution

**Core Principle**: This standard does not distinguish positions by seniority. The labor time of different roles is converted at the same rate.

Contributions may come in many forms—**labor time, capital, production materials**, and so on—and are uniformly converted into **Contribution Points**.

| Type | Formula |
|---|---|
| Labor time | 1 hour = 1 point |
| Capital | (Amount ÷ Local Hourly Wage) × Capital Weight |
| Production materials | (Valuation ÷ Local Hourly Wage) × Capital Weight |

- **Capital Weight**: Recommended as 3 initially; the team may negotiate adjustments.
- **Local Hourly Wage**: Based on the project's location of execution; for global or unclear cases, use the global average hourly wage (specific statistical source determined by the team).
- **Individual Share** = Personal contribution points ÷ Team total contribution points (dynamically calculated)
- **Profit Distribution** = Total profits × Individual share

---

## IV. Task Posting and Assignment

1. **Anyone may post tasks**. Required: description, estimated hours. Optional: acceptance criteria.
2. **The task list is public; first to claim wins**. Multiple people may collaborate and share the hours.
3. **Completion timing**: Members manage their own schedule, as long as the task's agreed deadline is met.

The initiator or project manager may curate the task list. AI tools may also be used to help organize it.

---

## V. Acceptance Mechanism

1. **Completion Confirmation**: Confirmed by the worker themselves, or by the project manager / relevant role. If there are deliverables or items needing handoff to others, they should be submitted in an appropriate manner.
2. **Hours Adjustment**: Every adjustment is recorded by the platform. Members with concerns may communicate directly. If communication is blocked, the challenge mechanism applies.
3. **Challenge Mechanism**: Any member may challenge any task (including completed ones) at any time, triggering a simple majority vote. If the challenge succeeds: settle by actual hours → current task is terminated → the team decides whether to open a new task to continue the work.

---

## VI. Decision Mechanism (Recommended, Not Mandatory)

Handle in priority order:

1. **Module owners** decide within their scope.
2. When conflicts over resources or other matters arise, **project manager / product manager** roles arbitrate.
3. If disagreement persists, **simple majority vote** by the team decides.

---

## VII. Disputes and Fraud

- **General disputes**: The vote is final. Members who refuse to comply with the vote result automatically exit the team (their existing contribution points are preserved).
- **Task deadline disputes**: Handle in priority order — **Initiator → Product Manager → Project Manager → Majority consensus → Vote**.
- **Fraud handling**: Falsifying hours, misrepresenting valuations, or stealing others' contributions—if confirmed by **4/5 majority + public evidence**, all of that member's contribution points are zeroed out.

---

## VIII. Voting Thresholds and Catch-all

- **Modifying the Cooperation Vision**: requires **4/5 majority** agreement.
- **Matters not explicitly specified by this standard**: decided by team **simple majority vote**.

---

## IX. Intellectual Property and Ideas

- **IP and Copyright**: IP and copyrights contributed by members during the project are licensed only for use within this project. If such IP belongs to an individual member, the license automatically expires upon that member's exit.
- **Ideas**: As intangible assets, this standard cannot provide legal protection, but it can serve as a reference record—after a member proposes an idea and before implementation, if acknowledged by other members, it is entered into the record; in future ownership disputes, this record may serve as reference.

---

## X. Boundaries

### Standard's Scope
- **This standard governs**: contribution records, cooperation rules, exit / entry, dispute handling.
- **This standard does not govern**: legal entities, taxes, contracts, legal ownership of IP, nor platform-level identity, data persistence, or privacy.

### Settlement and Upgrading
- **Additional Investment**: Requires simple majority approval from current members; once approved, converted to contribution points using the capital formula and added to the co-creation record.
- **When a Member Exits**: They may take back the capital and production materials they contributed but which haven't been consumed, valued at the original investment amount. Labor time investment is non-recoverable.
- **Upgrading to a Company or Organization**: The contribution point ratio in the co-creation record serves as the basis for **proportional equity allocation**.

### Recommendations for Future Team Development (Non-binding)
- When incorporating, it is recommended to hire professional legal counsel to map the co-creation record to the formal equity structure.
